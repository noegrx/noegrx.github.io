Analytics

Markowitz’s method suggests not to diversify the portfolio too much to obtain the most efficient portfolio.

It should be optimal to invest only in 3 stocks in the portfolio, mainly in Amazon (67%) and a little in Alstom (18%) and L’Oréal (15%). Conversely, it is not optimal to invest in Carrefour and Vivendi stocks.

Value At Risk

Amazon has a lower VaR (95) and a higher price thanks to the current situation and this is the reason why we have to invest in mostly to obtain the optimal portfolio.

L’Oréal has the same VaR but graphs show more fluctuations, so the investment is moderate in L’Oréal shares.

However, Vivendi has the lowest VaR (90) but the share price is low and gradually declines, so we should not invest in.

Carrefour has a high VaR of 100 and the price is under the 100 basis. It is preferable to not invest in.

Alstom has the highest VaR (110) but the price remains stable and relatively high regarding the other assets. We can have this asset in our portfolio at a level of 18%.

Indeed, this combination of stocks provides a maximum return for a minimum level of risk.
